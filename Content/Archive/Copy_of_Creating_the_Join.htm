Creating the Job and Join

Overview

Your Job generates data representing customers and a list of state names. Now you need to join that information to create a comprehensive report and output. Your Job will look like this:

Create

Job

  1. Right-click on "Job Design" and click "Create Job":

  1. Enter "Filter_States" for the "Name" and "Filtering Customer / State" for the Purpose. Enter a Description of your choice and click "Finish":

  1. Enter the first component "tFileInputDelimeted" and enter "Customer (CSV)" for the Name below the Icon:

        

 

Enter

Customer

  1. Select the Icon and click "Component" to edit the "Basic settings". Click the "..." Icon behind "File name/Stream" to select the input file "C:\StudentFiles\Custs.csv" and change the "Field Separator" to "," including quotation marks:

  1. Enter another component "tFileInputDelimeted" and change the Name for it to "States (TXT)" then click "Component" and the "..." next to "File name/Stream" and select the file "C:\StudentFiles\States.txt" and then change the "Field Separator" also to ",":

  1. Enter a new component "tMap" to the Job:

  1. Right-click on "Customer (CSV)" then click "Row" and click "Main" and drag it to "tMap" and then select "States (TXT)" click "Row" then click "Main" and then drag it to "tMap" to join the components:

  1. The Job looks like this, look at the "yellow triangle" at each component, which indicates that "No schema has been defined yet":

  1. Select Customer (CSV)" and click "Component" the enter "1" for the "Header" because the first row contains the field names and then click "..." next to "Edit schema":

  1. Click the "+" icon at the bottom left and change the "Column" to "First" then click again the "+" icon to enter the following fields the same way: Last, Number. Street, City, State:

  1. When you have entered all the fields click "OK":

Edit

States

  1. Select the component  "States (TXT)" and click "Component" the click the "..." next to "Edit schema" and the click the "+" to enter a filed,  name it "StateCode" and enter a second named "StateName" then click "OK":

Enter

Output
  1. Enter a new component "tFileOutputDelimited" and name it "Output":

  1. Right-click "tMap" then click "Row" and "Output" to join these components and see the result:

Note: that the row is a Lookup, not a Main. You can provide multiple input sources for a tMap component, but only one can be the Main row. The others are Lookups.

  1. Select "Output" and click "Component" then click "..." next to "File Name" and enter "C:\StudentFiles\filter1_out.csv":
