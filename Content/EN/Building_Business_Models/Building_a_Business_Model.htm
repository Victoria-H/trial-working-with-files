Building a Business Model

Overview

Business models are important to plan, keep track of and communicate your project. The studio includes graphical tools for designing business models which are kept in the repository.

Create a Business Model

  1. CREATE A BUSINESS MODEL

    In the Repository, right-click Business Models and then click Create Business Model:

  2. GIVE THE MODEL A NAME, PURPOSE AND DESCRIPTION

    Enter CustCleanup into the Name box, and appropriate text into the Purpose and Description boxes. Then click Finish:

    Only the name is required, but good documentation is one of the points of a business model, so it is good practice to be thorough.

  3. NOTE THE CHANGES INT HE REPOSITORY AND THE PALETTE

    The name of the new business model appears in the Repository, a blank model appears in the design workspace, and the Palette changes:

    Rather than Job components, the Palette now displays shapes you use to build the model:

Add Shapes

  1. PLACE SHAPES IN THE DESIGN WORKSPACE

    In the Palette, click Input and then click in the design workspace to place the shape.
    Enter Customer Data into the text box:

    This shape represents the input file for customer data. Tip: If needed, use the grab points to enlarge the shape so that the label is entirely contained within the shape.

  2. ADD ANOTHER SHAPE

    Add a Data shape, labeling it State Codes.
    The second shape is the lookup table of state codes and names.

  3. PLACE SHAPES WITHOUT THE PALETTE

    Place the pointer over an empty area of the design workspace and pause until the quick access tool bar appears:

    This tool bar allows you to place shapes without using the Palette.

  4. ADD AN ACTION SHAPE

    Click the Action shape in the tool bar and then label it Join, moving it near the other shapes:

The Join shape represents the tMap component that performs the mapping and joining.

Connect Shapes

  1. USE INPUT/OUTPUT ARROWS

    Place the mouse pointer over the right side of the Customer Data shape until two input/output arrows appear:

    Drag the right-facing arrow to the Join shape and then click Create Relationship:

    There is an alternative way to connect your shapes. The next step shows an alternative method. Note: The relationship shown in the diagram above (and below) is not directional. Feel free to select Create Directional Relationship if you wish to be more accurate with the example's data flow.

  2. ANOTHER WAY  TO ADD ARROWS

    Hover over State Codes. When the input/output arrows appear, drag the output arrow anywhere into whitespace of the designer view. Release the arrow and a contextual menu appears. Click Create Relationship To and then click Existing element:

  3. CHOOSE THE ELEMENT YOU WISH TO LINK

    In the list, select Action Business Item Join and then click OK:

  4. CONFIRM THE CONNECTION

    A pop up window will ask you to confirm the connection, click Yes:

  5. AND A Terminal SHAPE

    Add a Terminal shape, naming it End and connect the Join shape to it. If you opted for directional relationships your Business Model should look similar to the following:

    Note: The shapes have names representing their suggested purpose, such as Action or Terminal, but feel free to use them as appropriate for your needs. There is no functionality behind the shapes and connections.  It is for modeling and planning only. Clearly the model built here is quite basic, but it can be more elaborate.

  6. ADJUST THE APPEARENCE OF SHAPES

    Click the Join shape and then click the Business Models view below the design workspace.

    Here is where you can adjust the appearance of the shapes, text size, color, alignment and font, etc.

    Experiment the different changes you can make in the appearance of the Join shape.

Add Note

  1. ADD A NOTE TO YOUR MODEL

    To add a note to your model, right-click in the canvas and click Add and then select Note:

  2. PLACE IT

    The Note will be displayed and you place it in your model.

    One of the purposes of a business model is to document a project, and the note is one mechanism for doing that.

  3. WRITE THE NOTE

    Replace the default text with a message about the project. Notice that the shape expands to accommodate the text.

  4. RESIZE THE SHAPE AS YOU WISH

    Re size the shape so that the text wraps:

  5. SAVE

    Save the business model.
